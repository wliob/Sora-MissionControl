1 What Is the Kanban Board?

The Kanban board is your project command surface — it shows every task across every project in lane-based columns. Think of it like a live Jira/Linear board, but for all your agents.

Your board has 7 columns (lanes). Cards flow left-to-right: Triage → Todo → Scheduled → In Progress → Review → Done. Cards can also go to Blocked from any lane.
2 Each Lane — What It Means
📋

Triage

New/unprocessed ideas. Cards here haven't been reviewed yet. 0 cards = nothing waiting.

📝

Todo

Approved and waiting to be scheduled. Ready to be picked up when capacity opens. 0 here = no backlog.

⏳

Scheduled

Planned for a specific phase or time. 7 phases in your pipeline — next up after Phase A completes.

⚡

In Progress

Actively being worked. Phase A is in here now, assigned to biscuit. Only one card should be here at a time per project.

🔍

Review

Work is done — waiting for YOU to inspect. This is your approval gate between phases. You check it, say "approved," it moves to Done.

🚫

Blocked

Something is stuck. 1 blocked card — the reset track. Click it to see why it's blocked and what needs to happen.

✅

Done

Completed and verified. 108 completed tasks across all projects. Scroll to review history.

3 How To Use The Board
▸ Orchestration: Manual ⚙ Filter 🔍 Search 🟢 Connected
1

Filter by Project

Click ⚙ Filter and type sora-missioncontrol-v2 to see only your 8-phase pipeline. This clears the clutter of 108 done cards.

Pro tip: Save this as a filter preset if available.

2

Click a Card

Click any card to open its detail view — you'll see the full spec, comments, events, and run history. This is where you read what needs to happen.

3

Check Orchestration Mode

The button at the top-left shows Manual or Auto.

  • Manual — cards sit until you say go. ✓ Current state
  • Auto — ready cards get automatically dispatched to agents. Dangerous if unchecked.
4

Read Attention Indicators

Cards show red "⚠" for attention-needed. This means: crashed, gave-up, blocked, or stuck.

Green ● = healthy. No icon = waiting.

🔑 The key insight: You don't need to check every card. Just look at In Progress to see what's running, Blocked for what's stuck, and Review for what needs your sign-off. Those three lanes are your daily attention surface.

5 What To Click & When
👆 Click a card
Opens detail view with full spec, events, comments, and runs. Read the body to understand what this card needs.
🔽 Drag a card
Drag between lanes to change status. Drag to Blocked lane to mark stuck. Drag to Done when verified.
⚙ Filter button
Filter by tenant (project). Type sora-missioncontrol-v2 to see only your pipeline. Clears the noise.
▸ Orchestration toggle
Top-left. Manual = you control dispatch. Auto = system dispatches ready cards. Leave on Manual unless I tell you otherwise.
📋 Card comments
Click a card → scroll to comments. Add notes like "Looks good, proceed" or "Fix the grid alignment." Sora reads these.
📊 View events
Inside a card: the event log shows every status change, dispatch, and verification. Provenance for everything.
6 Your Commands (How You Drive This)

You never need to click through menus. Just tell Sora:

"start Phase B" → Sora dispatches Office Screen to Biscuit, card goes In Progress
"approved, next" → Current card → Done, next card → Ready, dispatch
"review Phase B" → I show you the screenshot + what changed
"fix the [thing]" → Route fix back to lead, card stays in Review
"blocked: [reason]" → Card → Blocked, reason recorded
"status" → I post current board state + next action
"auto-pilot on" → Orchestrator cron resumes, phases self-drive between your reviews
TL;DR: You only need to check Review lane. When something lands there, look at the screenshot I post, say "approved, next" or "fix [thing]". Everything else flows automatically.
